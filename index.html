There are 4 seasons with each season being about 3 months each.

Summer is is the hottest of the four temperate seasons, falling after spring and before autumn. Typically when students have a break from school and take advantage of the warmer weather and longer days. Calling for dresses, shorts, and open toes shoes. Sunscreen is a necessity to protect from the suns strong UV radiation. Spring and "springtime" refer to the season, and also to ideas of rebirth, rejuvenation, renewal, resurrection and regrowth. Many flowering plants bloom at this time of year, in a long succession, sometimes beginning when snow is still on the ground and continuing into early summer. This weather calls for cozy sweaters or cardigans as the weather slowly begins to warm.

Fall, also known as Autumn. When the duration of daylight becomes noticeably shorter and the temperature cools considerably. Day length decreases and night length increases as the season progresses until the Winter Solstice in December.One of its main features in temperate climates is the striking change in colour for the leaves of deciduous trees as they prepare to shed.Falls calls for boots, scarfs, steamy cups of coffee in preperation of winter. Winter is the coldest season of the year,the lower altitude of the Sun causes the sunlight to hit the Earth at an oblique angle. Thus a lower amount of solar radiation strikes the Earth per unit of surface area. In winter it typically snows, this weather calls for heavy jackets, coats and boots to protect from the cold.
